Biographies
Daryl B. Shankland

Born 1953
  • University of Illinois, BS Communications , Summa Cum Laude, 1975
  • Broadcast news reporter and sales representative 1975 to 1980, WLRW Champaign, Il and WGEM Quincy, Il
  • Registered Representative Paine Webber 1980 to 1986
  • Registered Representative Smith Barney and predecessor firms 1986 to 2003
  • Branch Manager Smith Barney 1987 to 1996
  • Registered Investment Advisor Butterfield Capital 2003 to 2006
  • President Shankland Financial Advisors, LLC 2006 to present
Daryl has been guiding clients in their investment decisions since 1980, encompassing both good times and bad. Her broadcasting background has helped her communicate complex, quickly changing information to clients in a manner that they can understand. Over the years, Daryl has been a frequent commentator on financial matters on both television and radio. While at traditional Wall Street brokerage firms, she was quickly recognized for her leadership qualities. She served as branch manager of Smith Barney’s Quincy, Illinois office for over 9 years and left the company after attaining the highest designation of Senior Vice President. With her deep exposure to both management and client portfolio structuring, she understands how emotions can take their toll and how critical it is to trust your financial advisor.

With a responsive business structure that enables her to work quickly on your behalf, she can manage your money using her keen sense of who you are and what you would like your money to do for you. Daryl describes herself as a” paid professional truth-teller”. She is interested in working with individuals who want someone to guide them, but who in their own right take responsibility for the financial well-being of their families. Her honest assessment of where you stand financially could greatly increase your peace of mind. If you are a long way from reaching your financial goals, Daryl can help you map out a plan to get where you want to go. If you are in good shape financially, Daryl’s mission is to help you stay that way and maximize what you can pass on after you are gone.

Daryl’s friends usually become clients, and her clients usually become friends. They would be most likely to describe her as smart, driven, and dedicated but with a nice mix of friendly and creative. In her free time Daryl enjoys traveling, painting, singing in her church choir, and relishing time with her husband Richard, six grown children, and seven grandchildren. Daryl and Dick share their time between their homes in both Quincy, Illinois and Sapphire, North Carolina.



Alden M. Burnett III

Born 1961
  • Eagle Scout 1976
  • Illinois State University, B.S. Communications, 1983
  • Eagle Scout mentor & Assistant Scout Master
  • History from 1993 to 1997 as a Manufacturer’s Representative working as liaison between manufacturer’s and small business owners
  • V.P. & partner 21st Century Building Products 1997-2004
  • Peace Memorial Church youth group volunteer
  • Registered Representative since 2004, most recently with Prudential Securities
  • Series 6 Investment Company / Variable Products securities license 12/02/2004
  • Series 63 Uniform Securities Agent license 12/17/2004
  • Series 65 Uniform Investment Adviser license 09/20/2006
  • Series 7 General Securities Registered Representative license 03/05/2007
  • Life, accident and health insurance licenses 2004
  • Joined Shankland Financial Advisers, LLC November 2013
Alden has been helping individual clients and business owners accumulate and manage their investments since 2004. He holds a strong belief that proper insurance protection is a key component of any financial plan. His focus on insurance helps ensure that the money which clients are accumulating is not wiped out in a financial disaster. His experience allows him to utilize resources from innovative companies around the world. He can review the protection you have in place and provide you a solution using the most current, competitive products available.

As a young man Alden achieved the designation of Eagle Scout. He has applied those solid principles to all of his business dealings and believes that character is doing the right thing when no one is watching. He is active in his community and has served as an Eagle Scout mentor, assistant Scout Master and youth group volunteer. His personal passions are his wife Sue and two children, outdoor adventure and his music.

Whether it's mountain climbing with his son, shooting rapids with the family or playing drums in a local venue he never forgets that the peace of security can provide all families the ability to chase their dreams. In his presence you will know that you are being guided by someone who cares and will see to it that he does the right thing for you and your family.
